Background

Bangladesh is a South Asian country bordered by India on three sides and the Bay of Bengal to the south. It gained independence from Pakistan in 1971 after a war of liberation. The capital and largest city is Dhaka. Bangladesh has a rich cultural heritage, with Bengali as the official language. The country is known for its lush greenery, river systems, and the Sundarbans, home to the Royal Bengal tiger. Despite challenges like poverty and natural disasters, Bangladesh has made significant strides in economic growth and development in recent years. Brooklyn Technical High School, often referred to as "Brooklyn Tech," is one of the most prestigious specialized high schools in New York City. Located in the Fort Greene neighborhood of Brooklyn, it was established in 1922 and is one of the largest high schools in the United States by enrollment. Brooklyn Tech offers a rigorous curriculum with a strong focus on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM). Students can choose from various "majors," including computer science, aerospace engineering, and biomedical sciences. The school is known for its competitive admissions process, requiring students to pass the Specialized High Schools Admissions Test (SHSAT). Brooklyn Tech alumni include many notable figures in various fields, reflecting the school's long-standing tradition of academic excellence.
